Perhaps the most common thing I hear from new clients is some version of: "I've tried meditation, but I just can't do it. My mind won't stop." If this resonates with you, I want you to know something important: you are not failing at meditation. You may simply need a different approach.

The popular image of meditation, sitting perfectly still with an empty mind, is, for most people, neither realistic nor necessary. Meditation is not about achieving a blank state. It is about developing a new relationship with your thoughts, learning to observe them without being carried away.

If sitting still feels impossible, try walking meditation. Walk slowly and deliberately, feeling each foot connect with the earth. Match your breath to your steps. This practice is especially powerful for people with restless energy or anxiety.

If silence feels overwhelming, try guided meditation or sound-based practices. Listening to a singing bowl, gentle music, or a guided body scan gives the mind something to anchor to, making the practice feel more accessible and less intimidating.

Body-based practices like progressive muscle relaxation or yoga nidra (yogic sleep) can be profound doorways into stillness for those who struggle with traditional sitting meditation. Lying down is perfectly acceptable, despite what you may have been told.

Start small. Two minutes is enough. Set a timer, close your eyes, and simply notice your breath. When your mind wanders (and it will) gently guide it back without judgment. That moment of noticing you have wandered IS the meditation. Each return is a small act of choosing presence.

Be patient with yourself. Like any skill, meditation deepens with practice. There is no such thing as a "bad" meditation. Every time you show up, you are strengthening your capacity for awareness, compassion, and peace.
